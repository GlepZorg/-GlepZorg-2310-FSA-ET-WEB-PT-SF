Bears

Bears are powerful, omnivorous mammals known for their size and strength.

  1. Ollie

    2. Brown bears are found across Eurasia and North America, and our Ollie is an impressive Eurasian brown bear.

  2. Mona

    3. Sun bears, like Mona, have a distinctive, crescent-shaped marking on their chests.

Giraffes

Giraffes are the tallest land animals, easily recognizable by their long necks and distinctive spotted coats.

  1. Frankie

    2. Giraffes, like Frankie, are gentle herbivores that spend most of their day eating.

  2. Coconut

    3. Baby giraffes, known as calves, are born already standing, and ours is named Coconut.

Lions

Lions are majestic big cats, known for their social behavior and iconic manes in males.

  1. Mella

    2. Lion cubs, like little Mella, are incredibly playful and learn hunting skills through play.

  2. Karl

    3. Lions have a strong sense of smell, hearing, and night vision. This is how our Karl always knows when dinner is served.

Monkeys

Monkeys are intelligent, agile primates with diverse species found in various habitats around the world.

  1. Cookie

    2. Spider monkeys, like Cookie, have incredibly long, prehensile tails for grasping branches.

  2. Earl

    3. Howler monkeys, like Earl, are known for their powerful and far-reaching vocalizations.

  3. Banana Pudding

    4. Squirrel monkeys, such as Banana Pudding, are agile and acrobatic, leaping between trees.

Alligators

Alligators are large, reptilian predators found in freshwater environments, characterized by their armored bodies and powerful jaws.

  1. Wren

    2. Baby alligators, like Wren, are hatched from eggs and are incredibly vulnerable.

  2. Aspen

    3. Chinese alligators, such as Aspen, are critically endangered and found in a limited range.

  3. Mika

    4. They have been around for millions of years, with ancestors dating back to the time of the dinosaurs. Mika has been around for 100 years!
